AI for Science

I currently lead the AI for Materials research team at Air Liquide, where we leverage advanced AI models to design new materials from small molecules to polymers.
My prior research at UPenn focused on using machine learning to probe hidden physics in complex systems, such as revealing the physical meaning of activation volume in the Eyring Model proposed in 1936.

  • Language Model for Material Discovery
  • Structure-dependent Eyring Model
  • Understanding Creep Suppression Mechanism with AI

Featured Project: StEP Model for Disordered Solids Ductility (PNAS 2023)

We propose the StEP model, the first universal theory to interpret the brittle-to-ductile transition in disordered solids. While previous theories are system-specific, StEP integrates machine-learned structural information to decode this transition across diverse glassy materials.

Featured Project: Is Grokking a Computational Glass Relaxation? (NeurIPS 2025 Spotlight)

By framing grokking as a computational glass relaxation, this work explains grokking from the perspective of Boltzmann entropy and proposes a physics-based grokking-resistant optimizer.

Science for AI

Applying principles from science (like statistical physics) to help understand and improve artificial intelligence. We explore the theoretical limits of AI learning and the dynamics of modern neural networks optimization landscapes.

  • High-entropy Advantage in Neural Networks' Generalizability
  • Grokking as a Computational Glass Relaxation
  • WanD: an Optimizer Learning Beyond the Loss Gradient Descent

Computational Materials

Discovering and investigating novel materials via high-throughput molecular dynamics simulations. Focus areas include membrane, polymers composites, and disordered solids.

  • Gas Separation Membranes
  • Polymer Nanocomposites (PNCs)
  • Disordered Solids

Featured Project: Bound Layer Dynamics in PNCs

Use MD simulation with explicit / implicit solvent and first characterize the bound layer formation via the change of polymer density, coordination number, and entanglements among bound polymers.

About Me

I am an interdisciplinary scientist specializing in Artificial Intelligence and Condensed Matter Physics. Currently, I am a Senior Research Scientist in the Data Science group at Air Liquide, where I lead the AI for Materials research team.

Prior to this, I obtained my PhD in Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering from the University of Pennsylvania, exploring hidden physics in disordered materials via machine learning and molecular simulation. Besides research, I am an active volunteer for the American Physical Society (APS), serving as an APS Career Mentoring Fellow (2023–present) and a member of the DPOLY Publicity Committee (2024). I also co-founded the UPenn APS Chapter and served as the Treasurer (2022).
